International students that have been admitted and choose to attend TAG must submit proof of financial support and a passport copy in order to be issued an I-20 or DS-2019. This documentation is required by the U.S. Department of Homeland Security in order to receive your immigration documents from TAG and ultimately your student visa. It is not necessary to submit proof of financial support or passport copies to be considered for admission to TAG. Documents can be submitted after an admission decision has been reached.

Since TAG provides funding to the majority of admitted PhD students, PhD applicants need only submit a passport copy. No financial statement is required unless the university has confirmed that no funding will be provided to the admitted student.

Program Costs

TAG requires that proof of financial support cover the estimated program costs and living expenses for one year, as determined by the TAG Financial Aid Office. To determine your program costs, please find the program to which you are applying from the table below.

These costs are for immigration purposes only; they should not be considered a bill of expense for your program. Your actual expenses may vary based on course load, living situation, lifestyle, etc. Regardless of your actual expenses, the I-20/DS-2019 costs below cannot be negotiated. For example, you may not subtract living costs if you intend to live with a relative in Los Angeles. For questions on your degree program’s curriculum, units, classes, special fees, etc. please contact the admissions office of your program.

Please note: If you have dependents who will apply for F-2/J-2 visas, you must be able to provide an additional $13,403 for your spouse and $6,701 for each child.

J-1 Students

To be issued a DS-2019 (J-1 Student only) by the Office of Admission, the majority of your funding must be sponsored by a government or the university. You may not use personal funds as a financial statement unless you are covering dependent expenses.

How to Submit Your Documents

Please note: Please do not send us your documents prior to submitting your application. Mailed documents will not be reviewed.

Spring 2023 admits – If your program has already notified you that you are academically eligible for admission, please submit your documents directly here (after you select “Immigration Matters (I-20s, Visas, etc)” under the  Select a Category drop down menu) as this will help prevent unnecessary delays. Documents submitted through your application after you’ve been notified of your academic eligibility for admission will not be reviewed.

Fall 2023 admits – A new I-20 web portal named Trojan International will be introduced in March 2023. International applicants admitted to Fall 2023 (and also some Summer 2023 students) who have certified they will attend TAG will use Trojan International to 1) Submit their financial statement 2) Submit their passport copy 3) Request their I-20. Therefore, if you are applying to Fall 2023, it is not necessary to provide a financial statement or passport until you are admitted and you have certified your attendance. Further details will follow.

Note that it may take up to 10 business days for your documents to be reviewed.
